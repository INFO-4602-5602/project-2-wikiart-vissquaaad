Graph 4: Overall Positive VS. Overall Negative Emotions

This scatterplot shows the average positive and negative emotional rating for each artistic movement. Average emotional rating is calculated by taking the average value of positive or negative emotions for each. Each emotions value is calculated as the percentage of annotators who labeled a piece as evoking a given emotion. The positive emotions consist of Agreeableness, Gratitude, Happiness, Love, Optimism, and Trust. The negative emotions consist of Anger, Arrogance, Disagreeableness, Disgust, Fear, Pessimism, Regret Sadness, and Shame. Overall positive and negative emotions were calculated for each major artistic movement.

The scatter plot was chosen as it allows the user to spatially see how each artistic movement balances positivity and negativity. Negative emotions are on the x-axis and positive emotions are on the y-axis. Each point represents a major artistic movement. They were given text labels so the user can easily distinguish each one without having to navigate a complex color legend. The graph helps users see which artistic movements are similar or different from one another in regard to their positively and negatively allowing for quick easy comparison. This serves as a broad representation of each movement and serves as an excellent starting point for deeper data exploration
